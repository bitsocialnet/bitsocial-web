# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Bitsocial Web primarily serves builders, community owners, open-source contributors, protocol researchers, and early users evaluating whether Bitsocial is a serious peer-to-peer social stack. They arrive from docs, GitHub, ecosystem apps, or search results and need to understand the protocol quickly enough to decide whether to read the docs, try an app, run infrastructure, submit a project, or follow the roadmap.

## Product Purpose

Bitsocial Web is the public surface for Bitsocial: the about site, docs gateway, app directory, and stats entry point for `bitsocial.net`. It explains Bitsocial as an open-source peer-to-peer network for social apps with no required platform backend, no global ban authority, and cryptographic ownership for users and communities. Success means the site makes the technical thesis legible, routes builders to docs and source code, and makes the growing app ecosystem feel real.

## Brand Personality

Technical, independent, builder-first. The voice should feel direct and confident, with enough protocol specificity to avoid generic Web3 marketing. It can be ambitious, but it should ground ambition in concrete primitives: peers, nodes, swarms, community ownership, anti-spam challenges, apps, RPC, and open source code.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic crypto landing pages with neon excess, token-price energy, vague decentralization slogans, or empty "community-owned" claims. Avoid SaaS template layouts that reduce everything to identical icon cards and hero metrics. Avoid consumer-social gloss that hides the protocol mechanics, and avoid dense protocol documentation on the home route where a builder should get the thesis first.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the thesis, then prove it. The first screen should make the core claim clear, and the sections that follow should provide concrete tradeoffs, apps, docs, and roadmap evidence.
2. Make technical freedom feel usable. The interface should translate peer-to-peer and self-hosting concepts into decisions a builder or community owner can act on.
3. Treat the ecosystem as proof. App cards, mirrors, docs links, and stats are not decoration; they demonstrate that Bitsocial is becoming a practical network.
4. Stay sharp, not loud. Use contrast, motion, and blue glow as emphasis, but keep the overall surface calm enough for reading and repeated navigation.
5. Preserve graceful access. No-JS fallbacks, reduced-motion handling, translation coverage, and responsive layouts are part of the brand promise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The site should target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls, preserve keyboard focus visibility, and maintain usable no-JS paths for core navigation and app discovery. Motion should respect reduced-motion preferences, and graphics should degrade to stable fallback images when device capability or browser support is limited. Translation and layout work must account for long labels, RTL languages, and mobile touch targets.
